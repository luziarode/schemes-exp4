Let us now consider again two specific aspects of fairness.

First, a payment mechanism may involve fair and balanced procedures, although the resulting distribution of payments to different workers may not be fair. That is what we call procedural fairness.

Second, a payment mechanism may result in a distribution of payments to different workers that can be considered fair, irrespective of whether the procedures that led to the outcomes were fair. This is what we call outcome fairness.

We are interested in how people think about the described piece rate mechanism in terms of these two fairness aspects.
